Homework 4: Social Campaign - Photomontage

Overconsumption Kills!

Layers Screenshots

In my photomontage, I am exploring the concept of overconsumption, specifically in the fast fashion realm. Every second we spend scrolling through our phones, we’re bombarded with thousands of videos, most of which focus on the flow of fashion. Every passing second we are met with what is “in” and what is “out”, obsessively following every fleeting trend and giving in to our materialistic desires. But keeping up with these standards requires money. This is where the fast fashion industry comes into play. Fast fashion offers consumers the luxury of shopping for as many ‘popular’ clothing items as they want, for insanely low prices. But just as easy as it is to make these purchases ‘guilt-free’, consumers can recklessly discard them. What we are left with, instead, are heaps of abandoned, worn-once clothing items that create waste in landfills. My photomontage takes all this information, and attempts to carry out the message of prevention: Say No To Fast Fashion! Buy Less!

Some techniques I used in my photomontage are blending modes, color adjustment, and layer masking. Looking first at blending modes, I made two copies of the “Buy Less, Choose Well, Make It Last” lettering, and placed one under “Pin Light” to make bold blocks with transparent letters, and the other under “Divide” to make bold letters but transparent blocks. Then I messed around with the colors and opacity until I got a graphic visual I enjoyed. In my color adjustments, I edited the Hue/Saturation, Exposure, Brightness/Contrast, and Selective Colors of certain layers I wanted to bring more urgent attention to. It also helped fix some of the images that were just too dark or bland to understand. Finally, in my layer masking, I attempted to cut out as many logos, wordings, and pictures as possible to stack together like a collage.
